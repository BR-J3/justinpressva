🐾 Eve / DAHS — Post 9

Eve. A Deadline. No Answer.

Eve was a 12-year-old dog surrendered to the Danville Area Humane Society on April 10, 2026. She had a microchip — registered to the Lynchburg Humane Society, who had placed her with her adopting family in November 2025. DAHS staff scanned her three times and reported no registration found. Lynchburg Humane Society located the registration in five minutes. Eve was euthanized on April 28, 2026. Lynchburg was never called.

Records retention deadline: May 28, 2026 — Thursday

On May 22, Justin Press VA identified a specific date in Virginia's records retention schedule governing animal shelter intake and euthanasia documentation. That date is May 28, 2026 — two days from today. A press inquiry was submitted to Senator Bill Stanley's office referencing that deadline directly.

No response has been received.

DAHS has not responded. The City of Danville has not responded. Senator Stanley's office has not responded.

Senator Stanley reviewed hundreds of pages of DAHS documentation. He stated publicly that his office was taking action. He pledged legislation. He said he would work to shut down DAHS unless improvements were made. His office has not stated publicly — as of today — whether the records he reviewed, or the records still held by DAHS, will be preserved past Thursday.

That is not an accusation. It is the documented gap in the public record as of May 26, 2026.

The City of Danville, through City Manager Ken Larking, previously stated that DAHS leadership had kept the city informed from the outset and that the city's contract with DAHS remains in good standing. The city has not addressed records retention.

On Thursday, May 28, one of two things will be true. Either the records that document what happened to Eve — and every animal whose intake followed the same process — will be preserved. Or they will not. The public will know which by the presence or absence of a documented response.

The public record is the public record. Make of it what you will.
