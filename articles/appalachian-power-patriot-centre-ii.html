The Buyer Was Unnamed. The Vote Was Yesterday. The Record Is This.

Last night this page asked a simple question. 1,200 acres of Henry County land. An unnamed buyer. A vote scheduled for today.

The public deserved to know before the vote. Not after.

Today the answer was confirmed. Henry County announced the sale of Patriot Centre II — the Bryant Property — to Appalachian Power Company. No development plans announced. No public disclosure before the vote.

Here is what the public record does show.

Appalachian Power already operates an electrical substation inside the existing Patriot Centre Industrial Park. In June 2025, Appalachian Power was the named infrastructure partner in Virginia’s first data center project on the AEP system — Google’s land purchase at Botetourt Center at Greenfield. AEP was described as instrumental in securing that opportunity.

In May 2024, Henry County signed a 50-year water storage agreement with the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers securing 3 million gallons per day from Philpott Lake via the Smith River — specifically to support future industrial growth. The county secured the water before the land was sold. The land was sold before any plans were announced. The public was informed after the vote.

On March 24, 2025, Appalachian Power filed Virginia docket PUR-2025-00057 with the State Corporation Commission — proposing new terms for very large electric loads specifically including data centers — requiring minimum contracts of 12 years, a 5-year notice requirement to leave, and an 80 percent monthly minimum billing demand. That filing was made one month before the Henry County IDA signed the purchase option agreement with an undisclosed company for this same 1,200 acres.

In June 2025, Appalachian Power was the named infrastructure partner in Virginia’s first data center project on the AEP system. By February 2026 Google had filed plans for three data center buildings on that site drawing power through Appalachian Power’s grid.

That is the documented precedent for what Appalachian Power does when positioned as the infrastructure partner for a large industrial site.

The community has asked what this land will become. That question has not been answered. What has been answered is the sequence of decisions made before the public was told anything.

The public record is the public record. Make of it what you will.
